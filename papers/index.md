# Papers

## [Two modellings for theory change](Grove_1988_TwoModellingsTheoryChange/notes.md)  (belief-revision, agm, sphere-semantics)
Grove gives two formal modellings for AGM-style theory change: systems of spheres over maximal consistent extensions and sentence-ordering relations.
The paper proves representation theorems connecting those models to Gardenfors revision postulates and to partial-meet contraction.
For this project it provides the clearest model-theoretic target for implementing AGM revision over finite worlds, spheres, or total preorders.

## [New operators for theory change](Hansson_1989_NewOperatorsTheoryChange/notes.md)  (belief-revision, agm, belief-bases)
Hansson generalizes theory change beyond closed AGM theories and single-sentence inputs by defining contraction operators over arbitrary expression sets.
The paper introduces simple and composite meet contractions plus minimal contractions that preserve useful non-closed base information.
For this project it supports a belief-base architecture where source expressions, deductive closure, set-valued contraction inputs, and revision policy are separate surfaces.
