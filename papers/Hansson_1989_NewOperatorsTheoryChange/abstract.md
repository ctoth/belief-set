Hansson argues that standard AGM theory change is too narrow because it operates on deductively closed theories and usually changes them by one sentence at a time. The paper generalizes contraction to arbitrary sets of expressions and set-valued inputs, then defines simple/composite meet contractions and full/partial minimal contractions. The central implementation lesson is that belief-base systems should preserve source-level representation separately from deductive closure, with full AGM theory contraction treated as a special case rather than the only architecture.
